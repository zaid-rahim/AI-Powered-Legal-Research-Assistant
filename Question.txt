Category 1 — Direct Case Lookup
The system can find complete details of any case using its case ID.
Examples:

“Tell me about Crl.A.1”
“What was the final decision in Crl.A.76?”
“Give me the full details of Criminal Appeal No. 49/2012”
It provides full case details, including the parties, judge, PPC sections, decision, legal reasoning, and full case text.

Category 2 — Section-Based Questions
The system can find all cases related to a specific section of the Pakistan Penal Code (PPC).
Examples:

“Which cases involved Section 302 PPC?”
“Which cases involved Section 324 PPC?”
“How does the court interpret Section 302(b)?”
It shows all relevant cases along with their facts and outcomes.

Category 3 — Person-Based Questions
The system can find cases involving a specific person, whether they are a petitioner, respondent, or complainant.
Examples:

“Which cases involved Muhammad Tahir?”
“Was Gul Hussain the complainant in any case?”
“Cases involving Zarin Khan”
It lists all cases where that person is involved and explains their role and the result of the case.

Category 4 — Judge-Based Questions
The system can find all cases decided by a particular judge.
Examples:

“Cases heard by Justice Ijaz Ahmed”
“Which cases were decided by Justice Amir Hani Muslim?”
It provides summaries and decisions of the cases handled by that judge.

Category 5 — Decision-Based Questions
The system can filter cases based on the final decision.
Examples:

“Cases where the appeal was dismissed”
“Cases where the accused was acquitted”
“Cases resulting in death sentence”
“Cases involving conviction”
It groups and shows cases based on their outcomes.

Category 6 — Analytical / Legal Reasoning Questions
The system can analyze multiple cases and explain how courts handle specific legal issues.
Examples:

“How does the court evaluate confessional statements?”
“How does the court treat delay in FIR?”
“What legal standard does the court apply for eyewitness testimony?”
“How does the court decide on bail in murder cases?”
It explains the legal principles and reasoning used by the courts across different cases.

Note:Category 5 and 6 are still not very much accurate.
